## Content philosophy

- Story-first. Structure serves the story, not the other way around.
- Loose skeleton only. Markizano riffs between anchors — do not script
  sentences, do not over-specify. Give him the bones, he brings the muscle.
- Energy comes through. If the structure feels mechanical, the video will too.
  Keep it alive.
- No fluff. Intros should be 5-8 seconds. Get to the point fast and stay
  there.

## For TikTok (60-300 seconds)

**HOOK** (First 5-8s): The hook line, possibly refined for spoken delivery.

**SETUP** (next 15-20s): One or two sentences establishing the tension or the before
state. Drop the viewer into the story immediately.

**INSIGHT** (next 40-45s): The core claim delivered through a specific moment,
example, or micro-story. One concrete thing. Not a list.

**LANDING** (next 30-40s): What changed. What the viewer should now see differently.
One sentence they can carry with them.

**PULL** (last 5-15s):
This is not a vanity CTA. It is a directional signal toward the
community Markizano is building.

A question or challenge that filters for the right viewer. Someone who nods at this question is exactly who KFT is being built for.

The question should make the ideal viewer feel *seen* — not just engaged.

The format should follow a story, not just list metrics. Markizano will read this and make an interpretation of this and each repetition may result in a different iteration. The goal is not to get every sentence nor word correct, but rather list enough bullet points that make a coherent segment he can follow without having to stop and reference notes. Make it easy on human memory.

---

## For YouTube (10-45min)

**COLD OPEN** (0-8s): Hook line delivered straight to camera. No intro, no
music bed, no title card. Start in the middle of something. Make the promise or claim first, then show evidence, and finally introduce in as few words as possible.

**ACT 1** — THE BEFORE (~5-10% of runtime): Establish the world before the
insight. The problem as it actually felt, not as it looks in hindsight.

**ACT 2** — THE INCITING MOMENT (~10-20%): What forced the rethinking. A
specific failure, a metric, a conversation. Make it concrete.

**ACT 3** — THE WORK (~50-60%): The actual technical content. Walkthrough,
code, architecture decision, debug session. This is the proof. Don't rush
it, don't pad it.

**ACT 4** — THE AFTER (~15-20%): What changed. Why the second version is
different. What you'd tell yourself before you built the first one.

**CLOSE** (~5%): One thing to try, one question to sit with, or a partial
thread that continues in another video. No formal outro needed.

## For LinkedIn

The text limit is 2000 characters. Stay below that and leave some space for the link to the post in question.

Always produce a LinkedIn teaser.

It should hook the user into wanting to read or learn more about the content.

## For Microblogs (like Twitter/BlueSky)

Output a 200 character or less hook to post to Microblogging platforms like Twitter or BlueSky. No need to generate a separate summary for both, the character limit and message is more important.

Always output a short-form with a link placeholder to the longer piece of content for microblogs.
